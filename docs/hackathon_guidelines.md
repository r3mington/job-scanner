# Call for Code AI: United Against Trafficking
## Hackathon Guidelines & Information

**Source:** [Austin AI Hub Hackathon](https://hackathon.austinaihub.org/)

---

### 1. Tracks

#### Track 1: Anticipate & Disrupt
* **Focus:** Detect, map, or predict trafficking risks before harm occurs.
* **Problem:** Trafficking networks leave patterns across digital platforms, financial systems, and global supply chains that AI can learn. Current tools rely on keyword filters and manual review that miss most signals.
* **Potential Solutions:**
  * Detect online recruitment (evolving language, fake recruiter networks, etc.).
  * Expose supply chain and financial exploitation.
  * Predict high-risk locations/conditions.
  * Map trafficking networks across platforms (connecting fragmented signals from social media, financial records, shipping, court filings, etc.).
* **Example:** AI agent monitoring job boards and social media across 40+ languages to flag coercive language patterns.

#### Track 2: Assist & Amplify
* **Focus:** Support survivors, caseworkers, and frontline organizations.
* **Problem:** Survivors face fragmented services, language gaps, distrust, and risk of re-victimization (80% risk without sustained support). Frontline organizations are under-resourced and overwhelmed.
* **Potential Solutions:**
  * Reduce caseworker administrative burden.
  * Break language and communication barriers (real-time crisis response, low literacy, coded language).
  * Connect survivors to services faster (shelter, legal aid, medical, employment).
  * Assess and reduce re-trafficking risk.
* **Example:** Trauma-informed, multilingual assistant helping survivors navigate resources.

#### Track 3: Accountability & Justice
* **Focus:** Organize evidence, strengthen legal work, and improve accountability.
* **Problem:** Prosecutions are extremely low. Evidence is scattered across jurisdictions and formats. Legal processes are slow, and systems re-traumatize victims.
* **Potential Solutions:**
  * Organize evidence across borders and formats (video, audio, financial, etc.).
  * Hold corporations accountable (analyze modern slavery statements at scale for compliance/greenwashing).
  * Accelerate legal research and case strategy.
  * Protect witnesses and victims during the legal process.
* **Example:** Cross-jurisdictional evidence correlator helping prosecutors connect trafficking cases.

#### Track 4 / Special Recognition: Art Against Trafficking
* **Focus:** Use AI-assisted storytelling, visualization, or media to move people from awareness to action.
* **Potential Solutions:**
  * Tell trafficking stories with dignity and power while respecting survivor experience.
  * Make the invisible visible via interactive data visualizations or immersive experiences.
  * Educate and activate audiences.
  * Amplify survivor voices while protecting identity.
* **Example:** Survivor-informed interactive data visualization showing trafficking patterns.

### 2. Submission Requirements
Submissions must be submitted by **July 9, 2026, 11:59 PM CT**.
* **Details:** Project title, tagline, and 200-word description.
* **Narrative:** Problem statement + proposed solution + expected impact.
* **Ethics:** Explanation of how the solution centers survivors and respects consent.
* **Repository:** Public or private repository link (if private, add the judge account).
* **Demo:** Short demo video or live URL (≤ 3 minutes).
* **Optional:** Slide deck (PDF), screenshots, cover image.

### 3. Judging Criteria & Stages
* **Stage 1 - Technical Review (July 9–18):** Valid submissions are scored by 2–3 reviewers on:
  * Works as described
  * Originality
  * Technical depth
  * Ethics (Do No Harm principles)
* **Stage 2 - Panel Judging (July 18–27):** Top projects advance to a panel evaluating:
  * Impact
  * Feasibility
  * Survivor-centered design
  * Presentation
* **Prizes:**
  * **1st Place:** $1,250 + trophy + certificate + AI Hub Incubator (6 months).
  * **2nd Place:** $750 + trophy + certificate + AI Hub Incubator (6 months).
  * **3rd Place:** $500 + trophy + certificate + AI Hub Incubator (6 months).
  * **4th & 5th Place:** Entry into the AI Hub Incubator.

---

### 4. Ethical & Safety Rules (CRITICAL)
* **Do No Harm:** Follow UN Do No Harm principles.
* **No Live Operations:** Build tools, not live operations. Use publicly available, synthetic, or simulated data. Do not engage with live networks, scrape active criminal platforms, or contact suspected traffickers.
* **Disqualification Rules:** Any solution scraping facial images, re-identifying victims, or bypassing bot/identity protections is automatically disqualified.
* **Consent & Privacy:** Solutions involving survivor data must demonstrate consent-first design, privacy-preserving architecture, and human-oversight.
* **Transparency:** Be transparent about model providers and training data. Label any AI-generated media that could deceive viewers about real people/events.
